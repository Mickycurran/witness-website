Overview

Witness is a forensic evidence camera app that creates tamper-proof photos using blockchain verification. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about how we handle your data.

What Data We Collect

Data You Create

  • Photos: Captured from your device's cameras (back and front)
  • GPS Coordinates: Your location at time of capture (OPTIONAL - you can decline)
  • Device Information: Device model, iOS version, app version
  • Camera Metadata: Camera settings (orientation, timestamp)
  • Cryptographic Hashes: SHA256 fingerprints of your photos and metadata

Data We Don't Collect

  • ‚ùå Your name or personal identity
  • ‚ùå Contact information
  • ‚ùå Browsing history
  • ‚ùå Continuous location tracking
  • ‚ùå Social media profiles
  • ‚ùå Payment information (app uses Apple's payment system)

How We Use Your Data

Local Storage (On Your Device)

  • All photos and metadata are stored locally on your iPhone
  • You have full control over this data
  • We cannot access files stored on your device
  • Files remain on your device until you delete them

Cloud Processing (Firebase)

When you capture evidence, we send the following to our Firebase server:

  • Cryptographic hashes of your photos (NOT the actual photos)
  • Metadata hash (NOT the actual metadata)
  • These hashes are used to create a blockchain timestamp
We NEVER upload:
  • ‚ùå Your actual photos
  • ‚ùå Your GPS coordinates
  • ‚ùå Any personally identifiable information

Blockchain (Bitcoin via OpenTimestamps)

The cryptographic manifest hash (a combination of photo hashes and metadata hash) is timestamped on the Bitcoin blockchain.

  • This hash is public and permanent on the blockchain
  • The hash contains NO personal information, photos, or location data
  • It's mathematically impossible to reverse a hash to get the original data

Technical Implementation

Witness uses OpenTimestamps, an open-source protocol for creating verifiable timestamps on the Bitcoin blockchain. When you capture evidence:

1. Photo captured ‚Üí Hashed on device (SHA256)
2. Hash ‚Üí Sent to Firebase Function
3. Firebase ‚Üí Submits to OpenTimestamps
4. OpenTimestamps ‚Üí Anchors in Bitcoin
5. Proof (.ots) ‚Üí Returned to your device
6. All data ‚Üí Stored locally on your iPhone

This process:

  • ‚úÖ Proves your evidence existed at a specific time
  • ‚úÖ Is independently verifiable by anyone
  • ‚úÖ Doesn't require trusting Witness or any third party
  • ‚úÖ Uses the security of Bitcoin's decentralized network
  • ‚úÖ Costs nothing (we pay the minimal Bitcoin fees)

The OpenTimestamps protocol is open-source and can be verified at: opentimestamps.org
