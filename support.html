‚õìÔ∏è What is blockchain timestamping?
Blockchain timestamping creates an immutable proof that your evidence existed at a specific time. Here's how it works:
  1. Hash Creation: Your photo is converted to a unique cryptographic fingerprint (SHA256 hash)
  2. Blockchain Anchoring: This hash is embedded in the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps
  3. Proof Generation: You receive a .ots proof file that anyone can verify
  4. Tamper Detection: Any change to the photo changes the hash, breaking verification
Important: Only the hash is timestamped on the blockchain, not your actual photo or location. The hash contains no personal information and cannot be reversed to reveal your data.
